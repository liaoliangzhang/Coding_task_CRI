Data

The datasets in here were worked up starting in the sub-folder team_code in the team_code_R.Rmd file and then in the project sub-folder data_prep.

  1. cri.csv = model-level data, numerical values
  2. cri_team.csv = team-level data
  3. cri_str.csv = model-level data, string values

Note that Team 0 is actually the original study and hypothesis test on which these teams based their own work (Brady and Finnigan 2014). This is of potential interest for comparison, but removed in the final analyses.

Model Specification Codebook

Descriptives

Sample

Our sample is 72 teams including 161 researchers that submitted 1261 models. Of those teams, one never carried out the hypothesis test based on a failed measurement invariance test, another had all their 8 models fail to converge and one other had one model out of several fail to converge. The result was 71 teams with numeric results of their hypothesis tests derived from 1253 models produced by 161 researchers in total with an average of 2.244 researchers per team. There were 161 participants in 72 teams that produced 1261 models, average of 17.514 models per team.

There were 16 teams that considered their two independent variables (stock and flow of immigration) as two independent hypothesis tests and therefore submitted two subjective conclusions. Therefore, we have 88 observations (‘results’) among the team-level data that derive from 71 teams. With an average of 15.762 models per team-conclusion unit of observation.
